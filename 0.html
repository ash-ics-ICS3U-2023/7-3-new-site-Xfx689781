About China's Geography

China is located in the eastern part of Asia, on the western coast of the Pacific Ocean. China's terrain varies greatly, from high mountainous areas to desolate desert areas and flat fertile plains, which in turn lead to the great variation of natural scenery in China. Therefore, discover where nature in China is bigger, better, and more wonderful than anywhere else in the world. Here, we're going to introduce the mountains, famous water-bodies and some other interesting scenery spots to visit in China! Consider these natural landmarks in China for your next trip!!!

MOUNTAINS

The Yellow Mountain (Huangshan)

Known as "the loveliest mountain of China", the area is well known for its scenery, sunsets, peculiarly-shaped granite peaks, Huangshan pine trees, hot springs, winter snow and views of the clouds from above. Huangshan is a frequent subject of traditional Chinese paintings and literature, as well as modern photography. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of China's major tourist destinations.

It is worth mentioning that the area has diverse flora, where one-third of China's bryophyte families and more than half of its fern families are represented. The Huangshan pine (Pinus hwangshanensis) is named after Huangshan and is considered an example of vigor because the trees thrive by growing straight out of the rocks.

Mount Gongga

Mount Gongga, also known as Minya Konka and colloquially as "The King of Sichuan Mountains", is the highest mountain in Sichuan province, China. It has an elevation of 7,556m above sea level. This makes it the third highest peak in the world outside of the Himalaya/Karakoram range.

The area has diverse flora, where one-third of China's bryophyte families and more than half of its fern families are represented. The Huangshan pine (Pinus hwangshanensis) is named after Huangshan and is considered an example of vigor because the trees thrive by growing straight out of the rocks.
